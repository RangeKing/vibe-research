# Adjudication Guide

Use this guide when multiple candidate framings, experiments, or narrative paths have been proposed and one path must be selected.

## Goal

Converge toward:

- one primary direction
- one backup direction
- one concrete de-risk path
- one explicit decision trace

## Inputs

- research brief
- candidate directions
- paradigm or reviewer-risk audit
- de-risk plan
- evaluation protocol
- narrative skeleton
- optional adversarial review

## Two-round workflow

### Round 1: Diverge, attack, de-risk

1. Generate multiple directions.
2. Stress each direction with a paradigm audit.
3. Attach the smallest falsifier and kill criteria to each direction.

### Round 2: Converge, cross-review, validate, package

4. Narrow to two or three candidates.
5. Cross-review the candidates from different roles.
6. Validate the metrics, baselines, controls, and figure logic.
7. Package the chosen direction into a claims-evidence map and a short execution plan.

## Scoring dimensions

Score roughly from 0 to 5 on:

1. `Impact`
2. `Novelty`
3. `De-risk speed`
4. `Comparative advantage`
5. `Robustness`
6. `Storyability`
7. `Creative risk under control`

## Hard gates

Downscope the plan if any of these are missing:

- a best-case conclusion that says more than "improves by N%"
- at least one explicit falsifier with a failure threshold
- at least one killer figure concept
- at least one negative control or a clear reason why no negative control is needed

## Decision trace

Always leave a short trace that states:

- what candidates were compared
- why the chosen direction won
- what was rejected
- what evidence would change the decision

## Required final output

1. primary direction
2. backup direction
3. updated one-liner
4. claims-evidence map
5. two-week plan with kill criteria
6. decision trace
