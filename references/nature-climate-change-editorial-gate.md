# Nature Climate Change Editorial Gate

Use this gate for Nature Climate Change and similar Nature-family selective climate journals before scorecards, package-readiness claims, title/abstract rewrites, or cover-letter positioning.

## Desk-Rejection First

The default verdict is rejection until the package proves otherwise. Produce a desk-rejection memo before strengths, polish, readiness claims, or score increases.

The memo must state:

1. The strongest concrete reason an editor could desk reject the manuscript today.
2. The title/abstract claim most exposed to that rejection.
3. The figure panel an editor would expect to prove that claim.
4. The source-data row or method step that would be inspected next.
5. Whether the issue is fatal, major, moderate, or minor.
6. What evidence, figure redesign, or wording change would lift the cap.

## Article vs Analysis Forced Decision

If all three are true, default content type is `Analysis`, not `Article`:

1. the central metric is constructed, ordinal, diagnostic, proxy-based, or threshold-weighted;
2. most evidence comes from existing public datasets;
3. the headline claim is conditional on index design, thresholds, component weights, or source-data reconciliation.

To recover Article identity, the package must show at least two:

- a new dataset or measurement layer;
- a new validated method that changes field understanding;
- physical validation against observed outcomes;
- end-to-end uncertainty propagation through headline conclusions;
- a specialist-surprising result visible in a main figure;
- a mechanistic result not obtainable from a framework alone.

## Reviewer Panel Rule

For interdisciplinary high-impact packages, simulate at least four specialist reviewers plus one figure editor. Use the harshest unresolved specialist objection as the score cap.

Minimum panel for carbon-climate packages:

- carbon-cycle / global carbon budget
- terrestrial carbon and disturbance
- ocean carbon / marine biogeochemistry
- coastal blue carbon / geomorphology when relevant
- quantitative methods / uncertainty
- figure editor

## Display-Item Economy

Nature-family display slots are scarce. A six-figure main package must not spend more than one main figure on provenance, input coverage, or "we checked this" dashboards unless that display changes the central conclusion.

The central answer figure should appear by Figure 2 or Figure 3 unless the paper has a strong experimental sequence. Provenance-heavy figures usually move to Extended Data or Supplementary Information.
