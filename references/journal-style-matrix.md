# Journal Style Matrix

Use this matrix after the user has named a target journal or journal tier.

This matrix is heuristic guidance, not an author-guideline substitute.

## Nature or Science flagship journals

### Typical preference

- very clear broad significance
- relevance to cross-field readers
- compressed opening logic
- strong mechanistic, conceptual, or field-level importance

### Writing implication

- explain why a broad reader should care early
- avoid dressing up a narrow technical increment as a paradigm shift
- keep the final significance sentence bold but bounded

## Nature Communications or Science Advances

### Typical preference

- clear significance, but more domain-specific than flagship journals
- solid evidential depth
- stronger tolerance for slightly fuller explanation

### Writing implication

- keep the abstract fast and dense
- make the evidential chain feel complete
- allow discussion to carry more explicit limitation and implication language

## Cell family journals

### Typical preference

- mechanistic depth
- causal logic
- figure-to-figure progression
- biological insight with clear support

### Writing implication

- prioritize mechanism over broad slogan language
- make the results section cumulative rather than flat
- reduce claim scope quickly if mechanistic support is incomplete

## PNAS, eLife, npj, and strong specialist journals

### Typical preference

- focused but solid contribution
- clean argumentation
- clear boundary statements
- reliable methods and support

### Writing implication

- do not force flagship-style grandiosity
- precise scope and restraint often help more than inflated tone
- moderate breadth can be a strength when the work is technically or empirically complete

## Minimum check after a target journal is named

Always judge:

1. audience breadth
2. strongest selling point
3. largest mismatch
4. rewrite strategy
